What to do?

During the summer period, June-August you can expect a lot of sun-hours as you're moving closer towards the polar circle. The long nights pairs well with a tent and some fishing. However, remember to first visit a local tourist-shop and get your self a fishing card.

There are many hiking tracks you can take through the province. Why not discover one of Sweden's first national parks, Sonfjället and take a trip to the top? But be aware, if you're lucky, one of the forest's giants can be a sight to behold. As Härjedalen is Sweden's most highly populated area of the mighty brown bears Paw.
